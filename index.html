Where Runlayer fits into Spire's roadmap

Prioritized to the signals we found in Spire's own SEC filings, careers page, and product announcements.

Priority 1 · Governance & Control

One policy layer for four business units

Spire's FY2024 10-K disclosed a material weakness in disclosure controls, followed by two NYSE late-filing notices in 2025. With Federal, Weather & Climate, Aviation, and Space Services each shipping AI-assisted products independently, ad-hoc agent access is exactly the kind of oversight gap regulators and auditors flag next. Runlayer's policy-based access control (PBAC) and approval workflows give every business unit the same governed guardrails, with one auditable source of truth for the board and Audit Committee.

Priority 2 · Compliance & Security

Audit-ready trust for defense contracts

Spire is ISO 27001 certified and carries explicit ITAR / EAR / OFAC export-control exposure, and it's winning deeper defense work — the Missile Defense Agency SHIELD IDIQ and a Diehl Defence early-warning agreement among them — without a public FedRAMP or SOC 2 attestation yet. Runlayer's Trust Center and SOC 2 Type II program, paired with immutable audit logs on every agent action, give Spire a fast, credible way to answer the next government security questionnaire.

From one model to a fleet of agents

AI-S2S proved Spire can build differentiated, in-house AI on top of its data. The next step — agent-driven workflows across GPS-RO, GNSS-R, and RF geolocation pipelines, deployed to teams in 9 countries on one shared AWS footprint — needs visibility. Runlayer's analytics dashboard and deployment management give Spire's platform team one pane of glass across every model, agent, and geography.

A catalog for an already API-first team
